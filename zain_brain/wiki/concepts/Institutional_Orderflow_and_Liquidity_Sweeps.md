# Institutional Orderflow and Liquidity Sweeps

## Thesis
Institutional Orderflow and Liquidity Sweeps are critical components of smart money trading strategies. They involve understanding how large institutions manipulate market liquidity to create high-probability entry points.

## Structural Rules
- **Identify Institutional Orderflow:** Monitor for large volume movements and price action that indicates institutional activity. This often precedes liquidity sweeps.
- **Liquidity Sweep Execution:** Enter trades at identified liquidity levels where stop-losses of retail traders are likely to be triggered, creating optimal entry points.
- **Risk Management:** Never risk more than 1-2% of equity per trade. Adjust stop-loss to breakeven after partial profit targets are hit.

## Edge Cases
- **False Breakouts:** Be cautious of false breakouts that may mimic liquidity sweeps. Confirm with additional indicators such as volume spikes.
- **News Events:** Major news events can cause unexpected liquidity movements. Avoid trading during high-impact news releases unless the strategy accounts for it.
