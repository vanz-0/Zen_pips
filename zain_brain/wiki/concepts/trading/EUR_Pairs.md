# EUR Pairs — Institutional Logic

## Core Pairs
- EUR/USD (Primary), EUR/GBP, EUR/JPY, EUR/CHF, EUR/CAD, EUR/AUD

## Institutional Characteristics
- EUR/USD is the **most traded pair globally** — highest liquidity, tightest spreads.
- Strongly correlated with DXY inversely. When DXY drops, EUR/USD rises mechanically.
- European Central Bank (ECB) policy drives medium-term direction.

## Session Behavior
- **Frankfurt Open (07:00 UTC)**: Pre-London manipulation. Often creates the initial liquidity trap for the day.
- **London Session (08:00-12:00 UTC)**: EUR pairs see their highest volatility here. London often sweeps Asian session highs/lows.
- **NY Session**: EUR/USD follows USD direction. Use DXY correlation to confirm.

## Key Levels for EUR/USD
- Always mark psychological levels: 1.0500, 1.0600, 1.0700, etc. These are prime liquidity resting zones.
- Sub-pip precision: When providing signals, use the rightmost decimal level from the screenshot analysis (e.g., 1.06532 not 1.0650).

## ECB Decision Days
- Similar to FOMC but with a press conference 30 minutes after the rate decision.
- The rate decision causes the first spike; the press conference often reverses it.
- NEVER trade during the gap between decision and press conference.
