# GBP Pairs — Institutional Logic

## Core Pairs
- GBP/USD (Cable — Primary), GBP/JPY (The Dragon), EUR/GBP, GBP/CHF, GBP/CAD

## Institutional Characteristics
- GBP/USD ("Cable") is the **most volatile major pair**. Daily ranges of 100-150 pips are common.
- GBP/JPY ("The Dragon") is extremely volatile — daily ranges of 150-250 pips. Only for experienced traders.
- Bank of England (BOE) policy and UK economic data (CPI, Employment) drive medium-term direction.

## Session Behavior
- **London Open (08:00 UTC)**: GBP pairs wake up aggressively. The first 30 minutes often create a fake breakout (inducement) before the real London move.
- **London Killzone (08:00-11:00 UTC)**: Primary window for GBP setups. Look for Asian session liquidity to be swept.
- **NY Overlap**: GBP/USD follows DXY inversion. GBP/JPY follows risk sentiment (correlates with equities).

## Sub-Pip Precision Protocol
- When providing GBP/USD signals, ALWAYS use the sub-levels on the farthest right of the screenshot codes.
- Example: Entry at 1.29432, not 1.2943. TP1 at 1.29876, not 1.2988.

## BOE Decision Days
- BOE announces rate decisions and publishes minutes simultaneously.
- Expect a 50-80 pip spike followed by a 30-minute consolidation, then the real move.
- If BOE surprises with a rate change, the displacement is usually 100+ pips in one direction.
