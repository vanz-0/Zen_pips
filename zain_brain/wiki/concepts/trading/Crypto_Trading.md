# Crypto Trading — Institutional Logic

## Core Assets
- BTC/USD (Bitcoin — Primary), ETH/USD (Ethereum), SOL/USD (Solana)

## Institutional Characteristics
- Crypto markets run **24/7** — no session closes, no gaps (except CME futures).
- Significantly more volatile than forex. BTC daily ranges of 2-5% are normal.
- Heavily influenced by **liquidation cascades** on perpetual futures exchanges (Binance, Bybit).

## CME Gap Theory
- Bitcoin CME futures close on Friday evening and reopen Sunday evening.
- The price difference between Friday close and Sunday open creates a "CME Gap."
- **80%+ of CME gaps get filled** within the following week. This is a high-probability institutional strategy.
- If BTC opens Sunday ABOVE Friday's close → expect a pullback to fill the gap (short bias).
- If BTC opens Sunday BELOW Friday's close → expect a rally to fill the gap (long bias).

## Weekend Manipulation
- Low liquidity on weekends makes crypto susceptible to artificial pumps/dumps.
- Institutions use weekends to accumulate positions at manipulated prices.
- **AVOID taking new crypto positions on Saturday/Sunday.** Wait for Asian Monday open for clarity.

## Liquidation Cascade Logic
- Monitor aggregate liquidation data (platforms like Coinglass).
- When $500M+ in longs are liquidated → price is likely near a bottom (smart money buying the liquidated positions).
- When $500M+ in shorts are liquidated → price is likely near a top.

## Correlation With Traditional Markets
- BTC increasingly correlates with NASDAQ/S&P500 during macro risk events.
- During Fed rate decisions, BTC tends to move WITH equities, not independently.
- Gold and BTC occasionally show inverse correlation during "flight to safety" events.
